WORLD & NARRATIVE

Make the map become a place.

The overhead map is authoritative. Player position and facing drive a matching first-person vector image selected from all nine current-terrain and next-terrain combinations.

05 / EXPLORATION

Seeded Map + First-Person Terrain Pair Renderer

Tap the overhead map to route through deterministic terrain, or turn and move from the first-person controls. The scene engine reads the tile beneath the player and the tile ahead, then loads the matching Forest/Lake/Grass vector composition.

How this was built+
A hashed seed feeds layered value noise and a typed terrain array. Bounded A* reads terrain costs. The first-person renderer performs a two-key lookup—current terrain plus terrain ahead—against nine explicit scene recipes, then constructs a native SVG image with reusable foreground and horizon layers.

07 / WORLD STATE

Quest & Faction State

Objective records dispatch effects into dialogue, factions, inventory, and exploration rather than maintaining disconnected checkboxes.

How this was built+

COMBAT & ECONOMY

Make every turn and target obvious.

Heroes occupy the left side according to formation. Monsters occupy the right. Commands are generated from the active unit, and every ability advertises exactly which allies or enemies can be selected.

A left-versus-right RPG battlefield inspired by classic menu-driven encounters. Hero placement reflects the formation board, active commands change by actor, and single-target abilities enter a clear ally/enemy target-selection phase.
